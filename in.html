Okay, so how this started?

Hello! I am Sushama Latkar-Satav. I teach synthesizer, piano, guitar and mandolin. I am an aficionado of Hindi film music. In my lifelong music journey, I am indebted to my loving parents (names) and my Teacher Prof. SuhasChandra Kulkarni (Institute of Modern Music). It is to their credit that I began the Kasturi journey. KMC was founded on 12th June 1990. One of my dear students’ Mother, Mrs. Sushama Koulagi, suggested the name ‘Kasturi’. Our first concert was at Balgandharva Rang Mandir. The programme was inaugurated by Shri. Shailendra Goswami who taught me Maths and Physics. Over the years, KMC has grown to a large, caring family of students and parents participating and helping out in all of KMC’s concerts.

The Kasturi Music Panache

Our style of playing the keyboard synthesizer incorporates both the hands. By playing the rhythm of the song with the left hand and the melody with the right, it gives a special lift to the song. We organize programs of our very own Orchestra, by playing songs in the above mentioned method, adding background music pieces and vocal singing. Pandit. Hridaynath Mangeshkar, Shri. Gajanan Watve, Kalyanji, Shankerji have graced our Institute and offered their words of wisdom. Hridaynathji gave us the golden opportunity to perform in his popular programme ‘Bhavsargam’. We also performed in the Doordarshan programme ‘Balchitrawani’ From 1990 to 2009, KMC has organized annual music gathering event to provide a performing stage for enthusiastic little wonders and ‘young at heart’ seniors as well.
